What is the stabilizer of a subspace W in a representation (V, ρ) of G?;The stabilizer is H := {g ∈ G | ρ(g)W ⊆ W}. It is a subgroup H ≤ G, and W is a representation of H.
Why does ρ(g)W depend only on the coset gH and not on the specific element g?;If g' ∈ gH then g' = gh for some h ∈ H, so ρ(g')W = ρ(g)ρ(h)W = ρ(g)W because ρ(h)W = W by definition of H.
How is the induced representation U = Ind_H^G W constructed from a subrepresentation W of H?;U is defined as the sum of all G-translates of W: U = Σ_{g∈G} ρ(g)W, which turns out to be a direct sum over cosets: U = ⊕_{[g]∈G/H} ρ(g)W.
Why is the sum U = Σ_{g∈G} ρ(g)W actually a direct sum over G/H?;Because ρ(g)W depends only on the coset [g], and distinct cosets give subspaces whose intersection is {0}, so the sum is direct: U = ⊕_{[g]∈G/H} ρ(g)W.
Why is the induced representation U = Ind_H^G W stable under the full group G?;For any h ∈ G and any summand ρ(g)W, we have ρ(h)·ρ(g)W = ρ(hg)W, which is again a summand of U (the one indexed by the coset [hg]).
What is the dimension of the induced representation Ind_H^G W?;dim(Ind_H^G W) = |G/H| · dim(W) = (|G|/|H|) · dim(W).
State the bound on the dimension of irreducible representations in terms of an abelian subgroup.;If G is a finite group and A ≤ G is an abelian subgroup, then every irreducible representation V of G satisfies dim(V) ≤ |G|/|A|.
Prove why every irreducible of an abelian group has dimension 1.;By the corollary of Schur's lemma, every ρ(a) is a homothety (scalar multiple of the identity), so every 1-dimensional subspace is stable, meaning all irreducibles are 1-dimensional.
Outline the proof of the dimension bound dim(V) ≤ |G|/|A| for irreducibles of G.;1) Restrict V to A; by Maschke it decomposes into irreducibles of A, each of dimension 1. 2) Pick W ≤ V with dim W = 1, a sub-A-module. 3) Form U = Σ_{[g]∈G/A} ρ(g)W ⊆ V; U is G-stable with dim U ≤ |G/A|. 4) Since V is irreducible and U ≠ 0, V = U, so dim V ≤ |G|/|A|.
What is the bound on the dimension of irreducible representations of D_n, and why?;Every irreducible of D_n has dimension ≤ 2, because D_n contains the cyclic subgroup C_n of index [D_n : C_n] = 2, so dim V ≤ |D_n|/|C_n| = 2.
How does an element g ∈ G act on the induced representation V = ⊕_{r∈R} rW (via coset representatives)?;Given r ∈ R, write gr = r_g · h with r_g ∈ R and h ∈ H. Then g sends the summand rW to r_g W, permuting the summands, while h acts inside W via σ(h).
What is obtained when H = {e} and W is the trivial 1-dimensional representation?;Ind_{e}^G W = ⊕_{g∈G} g⊗ℂ ≅ ℂ[G], the regular representation of G.
Describe the induced representation Ind_{C_4}^{D_4} W for the representation W of C_4 given by R·z = iz.;The cosets are G/H = {H, sH} with representatives {e, s}, so V = W ⊕ sW with basis {e₁, se₁}. The matrices are ρ(R) = diag(i, −i) and ρ(S) = [[0,1],[1,0]], giving the natural 2-dimensional irreducible of D_4.
What is the tensor product definition of the induced representation?;Ind_H^G W := ℂ[G] ⊗_{ℂ[H]} W, where ℂ[G] is viewed as a right ℂ[H]-module. The G-action is g·(t⊗w) = gt⊗w.
What is the tensor product V ⊗_A W of two A-modules?;It is the quotient of the free A-module generated by symbols {v⊗w} by the relations making ⊗ A-bilinear (elements of A can be moved across the tensor).
Give three examples of tensor products over a ring.;1) ℤⁿ ⊗_ℤ ℚ ≅ ℚⁿ (embedding integers into rationals). 2) (ℤ/nℤ) ⊗_ℤ ℚ = {0} (torsion elements vanish). 3) ℝⁿ ⊗_ℝ ℂ ≅ ℂⁿ (scalar extension from ℝ to ℂ).
Why is the choice of coset representatives R irrelevant for the induced representation?;The induced representation Ind_H^G W is well-defined independently of the choice of R; different choices yield isomorphic G-modules.
State the equivalence theorem between the two definitions of the induced representation.;Ind_H^G W = ℂ[G] ⊗_{ℂ[H]} W ≅ ⊕_{r∈R} rW as G-modules, where R is any complete system of coset representatives for G/H.
Outline the proof that ℂ[G] ⊗_{ℂ[H]} W ≅ ⊕_{r∈R} rW.;1) Decompose ℂ[G] = ⊕_{r∈R} r·ℂ[H] as right ℂ[H]-modules. 2) Tensor distributes over direct sums: ℂ[G]⊗_{ℂ[H]}W ≅ ⊕_r (r·ℂ[H]⊗_{ℂ[H]}W). 3) Each summand r·ℂ[H]⊗_{ℂ[H]}W ≅ W via rh⊗w ↦ σ(h)w. 4) Verify that the G-actions match via the map r⊗w ↦ ρ(r)w.
State the two main properties of the induced representation.;1) dim(Ind_H^G W) = dim(W) · |G|/|H|. 2) If K ≤ H ≤ G, then Ind_H^G(Ind_K^H W) ≅ Ind_K^G W (transitivity of induction).
What are the two ways to build representations of G from subgroups?;1) Descend: if N ⊴ G, a G/N-module becomes a G-module by composing with G → G/N. 2) Ascend: if H ≤ G, an H-module becomes a G-module via Ind_H^G.
State the formula for the character of the induced representation χ_V = Ind_H^G χ_W.;For every g ∈ G: χ_V(g) = (1/|H|) Σ_{s∈G, s⁻¹gs∈H} χ_W(s⁻¹gs).
Explain why only coset representatives r with r⁻¹gr ∈ H contribute to χ_V(g).;In the matrix of ρ(g) written in the basis ⊔_{r∈R}(basis of rW), the block corresponding to rW lies on the diagonal only when g sends rW to itself, i.e. gr = rh for some h ∈ H, i.e. r⁻¹gr ∈ H; otherwise the block is off-diagonal and contributes 0 to the trace.
What is the Hermitian inner product of class functions on G?;For φ, ψ ∈ C(G): ⟨φ, ψ⟩_G = (1/|G|) Σ_{g∈G} φ(g) · conj(ψ(g)).
Define the restriction Res_H^G V of a G-representation V to H ≤ G.;Res_H^G V is the H-representation obtained by composing H ↪ G →^ρ GL(V); it has character χ_{Res} = χ_V|_H (the restriction of χ_V to H).
State Frobenius Reciprocity.;Let H ≤ G, W a representation of H and U a representation of G. Then: ⟨χ_{Ind_H^G W}, χ_U⟩_G = ⟨χ_W, χ_{Res_H^G U}⟩_H.
What is the key change of variables in the proof of Frobenius Reciprocity?;Set s⁻¹gs = h ∈ H, i.e. g = shs⁻¹. Since χ_U is a central function, χ_U(shs⁻¹) = χ_U(h), so the double sum over (g, s) with s⁻¹gs∈H becomes a sum over all (s,h) ∈ G×H, and summing over s gives a factor of |G| which cancels.
Interpret Frobenius Reciprocity informally.;Inducing from H to G (going up) and restricting from G to H (going down) are adjoint operations: how much an induced H-representation "looks like" a G-representation equals how much the G-representation "looks like" the H-representation when restricted.
Verify using Frobenius Reciprocity that the natural representation V of D_4 is isomorphic to Ind_{C_4}^{D_4} W for W: R↦iz.;⟨χ_V, Ind χ_W⟩_{D_4} = ⟨Res_{C_4} χ_V, χ_W⟩_{C_4} = (1/4)(2·1 + 0·(−i) + (−2)·(−1) + 0·i) = (1/4)(2+0+2+0) = 1, so V ≅ Ind_{C_4}^{D_4} W.
What does ⟨χ_V, Ind χ_W⟩ = 1 tell us about the representations V and Ind W?;It means V and Ind W are isomorphic as G-representations (they share the same irreducible decomposition, and the multiplicity of V in Ind W equals 1).
What is the relationship between the regular representation and the induced representation from the trivial subgroup?;Ind_{e}^G (trivial 1-dim rep) ≅ ℂ[G], the regular representation, because there are |G| cosets (one per element) each carrying a copy of ℂ.
What does it mean for ℂ[G] to be a right ℂ[H]-module in the tensor definition of the induced representation?;ℂ[G] acts on itself by left multiplication by G (the G-action), while the right ℂ[H]-module structure is given by right multiplication: t · h = th for t ∈ ℂ[G], h ∈ H. This is used to define the tensor product ℂ[G] ⊗_{ℂ[H]} W.
How does the "twisting" by H appear in the tensor definition Ind_H^G W = ℂ[G] ⊗_{ℂ[H]} W?;Elements of the same coset gH act identically up to the H-action on W: if g' = gh then g'(t⊗w) = ght⊗w = gt⊗hw, so the H-part "twists" W while the coset part permutes the summands.
